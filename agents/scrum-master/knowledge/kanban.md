# Kanban

Kanban is a visual system for managing and improving workflow, originating from Toyota's manufacturing system in the 1950s. It emphasizes continuous improvement, efficiency, and transparency, and has evolved into a popular Agile project management framework.

## Core Principles

1.  **Visualize the Workflow:** Use a visual board with columns representing different stages of your process (e.g., "To Do," "In Progress," "Done"). Tasks are represented as cards that move across these columns as they progress.
2.  **Limit Work in Progress (WIP):** Establish a maximum number of tasks allowed in each column or stage of the workflow. This prevents overloading team members, reduces multitasking, and helps focus on completing tasks before starting new ones.
3.  **Manage Flow:** Continuously observe how tasks move through the workflow to identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies. Pay attention to the cycle time (how long tasks take from "in progress" to "done").
4.  **Make Policies Explicit:** Clearly define, publish, and share the processes and policies for how work moves through the system. This includes WIP limits, "Definition of Ready," "Definition of Done," and approval processes.
5.  **Implement Feedback Loops:** Establish mechanisms for regular feedback, such as daily stand-ups, replenishment meetings, delivery planning meetings, and retrospectives.
6.  **Improve Collaboratively, Evolve Experimentally:** Use data-driven decisions (metrics like cycle time, lead time, throughput) to gather insights and make informed decisions about process improvements. Introduce changes gradually and incrementally.

## Best Practices for Implementing Kanban

-   **Kanban Board:** Utilize a physical or digital Kanban board to visualize all work items and their progress through the workflow.
-   **Visualize Blockers:** Clearly mark work items that are blocked or waiting on external input to create awareness and improve transparency.
-   **Set WIP Limits:** Carefully set and enforce WIP limits for each stage of the workflow to optimize flow and prevent bottlenecks.
-   **Monitor Cycle Time:** Track the cycle time of work items to identify areas for improvement and predict delivery times.
-   **Define Policies:** Explicitly define the rules for moving work items through the workflow, including entry and exit criteria for each stage.
-   **Regular Reviews:** Conduct regular reviews (e.g., daily stand-ups, replenishment meetings, service delivery reviews, operations reviews) to inspect and adapt the process.
-   **Evolutionary Change:** Introduce changes incrementally and based on data, respecting current processes, roles, and responsibilities.

## Benefits of Kanban

-   Increased efficiency and productivity.
-   Greater flexibility and adaptability to changes.
-   Improved communication and collaboration within teams.
-   Higher quality output due to focused work.
-   Enhanced transparency of work and progress.
