# GitHub Profile Optimization

This document provides a strategy for turning your GitHub profile into a professional portfolio. The goal is to effectively showcase your skills and projects to support your primary career goal of landing a new role as an Automation Engineer (**G1**).

---

## 1. The Profile README: Your Digital Storefront

This is the single most important part of your profile. You can create a special repository with the same name as your GitHub username (e.g., `your-username/your-username`). The `README.md` in this repository will appear on your main profile page.

**What to Include:**

- **Introduction:** A brief intro. "Hi, I'm Rohi, a Cloud Security and AI Automation Engineer passionate about building efficient systems."
- **What I'm Working On:** Mention a key project you're excited about, like "...currently building a fleet of AI agents to automate my personal knowledge management (**Prtd4**)."
- **My Skills:** This is crucial. List the skills from your TELOS, especially from the **SdJ1** (AI Automations Engineer) and **SdJ2** (Cloud Security) sections. Use icons for visual appeal.
  - 🦾 **Automation:** n8n, Python, GitHub Actions, Azure Logic Apps
  - ☁️ **Cloud & Security:** Azure, Entra ID, Intune, Docker, Terraform
  - 🤖 **AI:** Prompt Engineering, AI Agents, LangChain
- **Contact/Links:** Provide links to your LinkedIn profile or personal blog.

---

## 2. Pin Your Best Repositories

You can pin up to six repositories to the top of your profile. These should be your best work, not just your most recent. They should directly reflect the job you want.

**Recommended Pins for Your Goals:**

- A repository showcasing your **n8n workflows** (**Prtt2**).
- The **secure-chatbot-ms-tenants** project (**Prtd7**), as it combines your security and AI skills.
- Your **Home Lab on Docker Swarm** project (**Prtt9**), once documented, to show DevOps skills.
- Any other project that has a fantastic `README.md` and clean, well-organized code.

**Action:** For each pinned repository, edit the "description" to be a concise, compelling summary of what the project is.

---

## 3. Demonstrate Consistent Activity

Recruiters and hiring managers look at the contribution graph (the grid of green squares) as an indicator of your passion and consistency.

**How to Improve Activity:**

- **Commit Regularly:** Even small updates to your projects or documentation count.
- **Document Your Journey:** As you learn and build more automations (**M9**), make small, regular commits. This shows you are consistently engaged in your craft.
- **Contribute to Open Source:** This is not essential but is highly regarded. Even small contributions, like fixing a typo in the documentation of a tool you use, show that you are an engaged member of the community.

By implementing these three strategies, you will transform your GitHub profile from a simple code host into a powerful tool for career advancement.
