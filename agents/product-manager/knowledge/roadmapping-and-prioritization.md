# Roadmapping and Prioritization for Product Managers

A product roadmap is a high-level visual summary that maps out the vision and direction of your product offering over time. It's a guiding strategic document as well as a plan for executing the strategy. Roadmaps can be feature-based (listing specific features to be built) or theme-based (focusing on high-level customer problems to solve).

Prioritization is the process of deciding which features and initiatives to work on next. It's a crucial part of product management, as it ensures that the team is working on the most important things first.

## How to Create a Product Roadmap:

1.  **Define your product strategy:** This includes your product vision, goals, and initiatives.
2.  **Gather ideas and feedback:** Collect ideas and feedback from customers, stakeholders, and your team.
3.  **Prioritize your initiatives:** Use a prioritization framework to decide what to work on next.
4.  **Create a visual roadmap:** Use a roadmapping tool to create a visual representation of your plan.
5.  **Share and get feedback:** Share your roadmap with stakeholders and get their feedback.
6.  **Review and update regularly:** Your roadmap should be a living document that you review and update on a regular basis.

## Common Prioritization Frameworks:

*   **RICE:** A prioritization framework that stands for Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort. The RICE score is calculated as: `(Reach * Impact * Confidence) / Effort`.
*   **MoSCoW:** A prioritization technique that stands for Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, and Won't-have. This method is useful for getting a clear understanding of what is essential for a release.
*   **Kano Model:** A model for product development and customer satisfaction that classifies customer preferences into five categories: Must-be Quality, One-dimensional Quality, Attractive Quality, Indifferent Quality, and Reverse Quality.
*   **Value vs. Effort:** A simple and effective way to prioritize by plotting each feature on a 2x2 matrix based on its value and the effort required to implement it. This helps to identify quick wins (high value, low effort) and major projects (high value, high effort).

## Common Roadmapping Tools:

*   **Aha!:** A popular roadmapping tool that helps you set strategy, capture ideas, and create visual roadmaps.
*   **ProductPlan:** A tool for creating and sharing beautiful, collaborative roadmaps.
*   **Jira:** While primarily a project management tool, Jira can also be used for roadmapping, especially when combined with other Atlassian products like Confluence.
