# Portfolio Diversification Strategies

This document provides foundational knowledge on investment portfolio diversification. Its purpose is to guide the user in building a balanced investment portfolio (**Prtd5**) to work towards their goal of financial independence (**M5**, **N14**).

---

## 1. The Goal: Don't Put All Your Eggs in One Basket

The core principle of diversification is to reduce risk by spreading investments across various assets. Different assets react differently to the same economic events. By holding a mix, you smooth out the ride; the poor performance of one investment is offset by the good performance of another. This is key to reducing the stress associated with market volatility (**C4**).

---

## 2. Key Diversification Strategies

### 2.1. Diversification Across Asset Classes

This is the most important form of diversification. The two primary asset classes are:
- **Equities (Stocks):** Higher risk, higher potential for long-term growth.
- **Fixed Income (Bonds):** Lower risk, provide stability and income.

The ratio between these (e.g., 80% stocks, 20% bonds) is the main driver of your portfolio's overall risk and return profile.

### 2.2. Diversification Within Asset Classes

Within each asset class, you should also diversify:
- **Geographic Diversification:** Don't only invest in your home country. Own companies from around the world (US, Europe, Asia, Emerging Markets). A war or recession in one country (**Pg1**) will have less impact on your total portfolio.
- **Sector Diversification:** Own companies across different industries (technology, healthcare, consumer goods, etc.).
- **Company Size Diversification:** Own a mix of large, established companies (large-cap) and smaller, high-growth companies (small-cap).

## 3. The Easiest Way to Start: ETFs

For an individual investor, buying thousands of different stocks and bonds is impractical. **Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)** solve this problem. An ETF is a single fund that you can buy and sell like a stock, but it holds hundreds or thousands of different underlying assets.

**Connection to TELOS:**
- Using a small number of broad-market ETFs is the most efficient way to build a diversified portfolio, aligning with your preference for smart and efficient systems (**N12**, **N23**).

### A Simple Starting Portfolio Example (The 2-Fund Portfolio)

This is a globally diversified, simple, and effective strategy.

1.  **Global Stock Market ETF (e.g., VT - Vanguard Total World Stock ETF):** This single ETF gives you ownership in thousands of companies across all sectors and geographic regions, automatically providing diversification within equities.
    - *Allocation Example: 80%*

2.  **Global Bond Market ETF (e.g., BNDW - Vanguard Total World Bond ETF):** This ETF gives you exposure to thousands of government and corporate bonds from around the world, providing stability.
    - *Allocation Example: 20%*

By holding just these two ETFs, you achieve a highly diversified portfolio that is low-cost and easy to manage. You can adjust the 80/20 allocation based on your personal risk tolerance. This simple, robust strategy is an excellent starting point for building your investment portfolio (**Prtd5**).
