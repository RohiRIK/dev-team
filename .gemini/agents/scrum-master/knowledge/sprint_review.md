# Sprint Review

The Sprint Review is held at the end of the Sprint to inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed. It is an informal meeting, not a status meeting, and the presentation of the Increment is intended to elicit feedback and foster collaboration.

## Purpose

The primary purpose of the Sprint Review is to inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed. It is a collaborative working session where the Scrum Team and stakeholders discuss what was done in the Sprint, what has changed in their environment, and what to do next.

## Key Characteristics and Best Practices

-   **Time-boxed:** The Sprint Review is time-boxed to a maximum of four hours for a one-month Sprint. For shorter Sprints, the event is usually shorter.
-   **Attendees:** The Scrum Team (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team) and key stakeholders attend the Sprint Review.
-   **Informal Meeting:** It is an informal meeting, not a status meeting. The presentation of the Increment is intended to elicit feedback and foster collaboration.
-   **Demonstrate Working Increment:** The Development Team demonstrates the work that it has "Done" during the Sprint and answers questions about the Increment.
-   **Product Owner Explains:** The Product Owner explains what Product Backlog items have been "Done" and what has not been "Done". They also discuss the Product Backlog as it stands, projecting likely completion dates based on progress to date.
-   **Stakeholder Feedback:** Stakeholders collaborate with the Scrum Team on the next things that could be done to optimize product value. Feedback from stakeholders is crucial for adapting the Product Backlog.
-   **Review of Timeline, Budget, Capabilities, and Marketplace:** The entire group collaborates on what to do next, so the Sprint Review provides valuable input to subsequent Sprint Planning.
-   **Focus on Outcomes, Not Just Output:** Emphasize the value and impact of the work, not just the tasks completed.
-   **Show, Don't Just Tell:** Demonstrate the actual working product rather than just talking about it.
-   **Gather and Document Feedback:** Encourage open and honest feedback, ask specific questions, and document all comments and suggestions for future reference and action.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

-   Turning it into a one-way presentation without active stakeholder engagement.
-   Focusing on problems or incomplete work rather than the value delivered.
-   Not preparing adequately for the demonstration.
-   Allowing the meeting to exceed its time-box.
-   Failing to capture and act on stakeholder feedback.
