# Daily Scrum

The Daily Scrum, also known as the daily stand-up, is a 15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team to synchronize activities and create a plan for the next 24 hours. It is held at the same time and place each day to reduce complexity.

## Purpose

The primary purpose of the Daily Scrum is to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog as necessary, adjusting the upcoming planned work. It is a key inspect and adapt event.

## Key Characteristics and Best Practices

-   **Time-boxed to 15 minutes:** Strict adherence to the time-box keeps the meeting focused and efficient.
-   **Consistent Time and Place:** Helps establish a routine and ensures all team members can attend regularly.
-   **Development Team Only:** Primarily for the Development Team. The Scrum Master ensures the meeting happens and teaches the team to keep it within the time-box. The Product Owner can attend as an observer.
-   **Focus on the Sprint Goal:** Discussions should revolve around progress towards the current Sprint Goal and identifying impediments.
-   **Three Questions (or adapted format):** Traditionally, team members answer:
    1.  What did I do yesterday that helped the Development Team meet the Sprint Goal?
    2.  What will I do today to help the Development Team meet the Sprint Goal?
    3.  Do I see any impediment that prevents me or the Development Team from meeting the Sprint Goal?
    Teams can adapt these questions to what works best for them, as long as the focus remains on progress toward the Sprint Goal.
-   **Identify Impediments, Don't Solve Problems:** The Daily Scrum is for flagging obstacles. Detailed discussions or problem-solving should be deferred to separate meetings, often immediately after the Daily Scrum (the "16th minute").
-   **Use Visual Aids:** Utilizing a physical or digital Scrum Board (task board) helps visualize progress, tasks, and impediments, making the discussion more concrete.
-   **Promote Transparency:** Foster an environment where team members feel comfortable sharing progress, challenges, and concerns openly.
-   **Self-Organizing Team:** The Development Team is responsible for conducting the Daily Scrum. It's their meeting to plan their work.
-   **Avoid Status Updates to Management:** The Daily Scrum is not a status report for management. It's a planning meeting for the Development Team.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

-   Allowing the meeting to exceed the 15-minute time-box.
-   Turning it into a problem-solving session.
-   Using it as a status update for the Scrum Master or management.
-   Focusing solely on individual tasks rather than collective progress toward the Sprint Goal.
-   Lack of engagement or participation from Development Team members.
