## 1. Foundational Movements

Based on your six years of experience with kettlebells (**N7**), you are already familiar with the foundational movements. Your current workout plan (**Wrk1**) correctly focuses on these core lifts:

- **The Swing:** The foundation of ballistic kettlebell training. Your plan includes both two-handed and double kettlebell variations.
- **The Goblet Squat:** Excellent for building leg strength and reinforcing good squat mechanics.
- **The Press:** A core upper-body lift for building shoulder strength and stability.
- **The Clean:** The essential movement for getting the kettlebells into the rack position for presses and squats.
- **Rows:** A fundamental pulling movement to balance out the pressing work.

Other key movements to incorporate for all-around fitness include:
- **The Turkish Get-Up (TGU):** A full-body movement that builds stability, mobility, and resilience. Your plan notes the Windmill as a TGU alternative, which is a great choice.
- **Carries (e.g., Farmer's Walk, Overhead Carry):** Builds grip strength, core stability, and work capacity.

## 2. Programming Principles

Your current plan already incorporates excellent advanced principles. This section is to reinforce and expand upon them.

### 2.1. Progressive Overload

**Principle:** To continuously get stronger, you must gradually increase the demand on your muscles. Your current plan's guideline to "Add 1 rep to top sets" or "Increase weight when completing 3 consecutive sessions" is a perfect implementation of this.

**Connection to TELOS:**
- This principle is the key to making workouts "smart and efficient" (**N12**) and re-establishing a consistent, effective fitness routine (**G2**).

### 2.2. Deload Weeks

**Principle:** As noted in your plan (**Wrk1**), taking a deload week every 4th week by reducing volume (not intensity) is critical for long-term, injury-free progress. It allows the nervous system and connective tissues to recover, leading to better performance in the next training block.

**Connection to TELOS:**
- This prevents burnout and aligns with the goal of making workouts enjoyable and sustainable (**M6**, **Fp5**).

### 2.3. Workout Efficiency

**Principle:** Kettlebell training is inherently efficient. By focusing on compound movements that use the whole body, you can achieve a significant training effect in a short amount of time.

**Connection to TELOS:**
- This directly supports your fitness principle of keeping workouts between 15-40 minutes (**Fp3**).

## 3. Sample Weekly Structure (Alternative)

This is an alternative weekly structure based on a common Heavy/Light/Medium template. It can be used as a variation to your current plan to introduce new stimuli.

- **Day 1: Heavy Day (Strength Focus)**
  - **Focus:** Low reps, heavy weight. Long rest periods (2-3 mins).
  - **A1:** Double Kettlebell Press - 5 sets of 3-5 reps (using 20kg KBs, **Wos2**)
  - **A2:** Double Kettlebell Front Squat - 5 sets of 3-5 reps (using 24kg & 20kg KBs)
  - **B1:** Heavy Single-Arm Rows - 4 sets of 6-8 reps (using 32kg KB, **Wos4**)
  - **C1:** Heavy Swings - 6 sets of 10 reps (using 32kg KB, **Wos4**)

- **Day 2: Light Day (Technique & Mobility Focus)**
  - **Focus:** High reps, light weight. Focus on perfect form and mobility.
  - **A1:** Turkish Get-Ups - 3 sets of 1 rep per side (slow and controlled, using 16kg KB, **Wos1**)
  - **A2:** Halos & Goblet Squats - 5 rounds of 5 halos (each way) into 5 goblet squats (using 10kg KB, **Wos5**)
  - **B1:** Light Swings - 10 minutes of 10 swings every minute on the minute (using 16kg KB, **Wos1**)

- **Day 3: Medium Day (Volume & Conditioning Focus)**
  - **Focus:** Moderate weight and reps, shorter rest periods (60-90 secs).
  - **A1:** Double Kettlebell Clean & Press - 5 sets of 5 reps (using 16kg KBs, **Wos1**)
  - **A2:** Single Leg Deadlifts - 4 sets of 8-10 reps per side (using 20kg KB, **Wos2**)
  - **B1:** Snatches - 10 sets of 5 reps per arm (using 16kg KB, **Wos1**)
  - **C1:** Farmer's Walk - 3 sets of 50 meters (heavy carry)
