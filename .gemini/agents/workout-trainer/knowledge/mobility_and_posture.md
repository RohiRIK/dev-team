# Mobility & Posture Improvement

This document provides a knowledge base for mobility and posture exercises, specifically tailored to counteract the effects of prolonged sitting and screen time (**Pr8**, **N5**). This directly supports the user's fitness principles to "Work on my posture" (**Fp1**) and "Work on my mobility" (**Fp2**).

---

## 1. Why Mobility Matters for Desk Workers

Prolonged sitting leads to predictable patterns of muscle tightness and weakness:
- **Tight Muscles:** Hip flexors, chest (pectorals), and hamstrings.
- **Weak Muscles:** Glutes, upper back (rhomboids, traps), and core.

This combination pulls the body into a slouched posture, which can lead to pain and reduced performance in workouts. A dedicated mobility practice is essential for longevity and feeling good.

---

## 2. The 5-Minute Daily Mobility Routine

This routine should be performed once a day, either in the morning to start the day or in the evening to decompress. It is separate from your main workout warm-up.

1.  **Cat-Cow Stretch:** 10 reps (Focus on moving through the full range of your spine).
2.  **Thoracic Spine Rotations (Quadruped):** 8 reps per side (Opens up the upper back).
3.  **Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch:** 30 seconds per side (Opens up tight hips from sitting).
4.  **Wall Chest Stretch:** 30 seconds per side (Opens up the chest).
5.  **Glute Bridge:** 15 reps (Activates weak glutes).

---

## 3. Key Corrective Exercises Explained

### 3.1. Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

**Why:** Sitting shortens the hip flexors, which can pull the pelvis forward and contribute to lower back pain. This stretch lengthens them.

**How:** Get into a lunge position with your back knee on the ground. Gently push your hips forward while keeping your torso upright. You should feel a stretch in the front of your hip on the back leg.

### 3.2. Thoracic Spine Rotations

**Why:** Desk work encourages a rounded upper back (kyphosis). This exercise promotes rotational mobility in the thoracic spine, helping to counteract the slouch.

**How:** Start on all fours. Place one hand behind your head. Rotate that elbow down towards the opposite wrist, then rotate it up towards the ceiling, following with your eyes.

### 3.3. Glute Bridge

**Why:** The glutes often become weak and inactive from sitting all day ("gluteal amnesia"). This exercise "wakes them up" and strengthens them, which is crucial for supporting the lower back and powering movements like the kettlebell swing.

**How:** Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Drive through your heels to lift your hips towards the ceiling, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower with control.

### 3.4. Wall Chest Stretch

**Why:** Leaning over a keyboard shortens and tightens the chest muscles. This stretch opens them up, allowing your shoulders to sit back in a more natural position.

**How:** Stand in a doorway or next to a wall. Place your forearm on the wall with your elbow bent at 90 degrees. Gently step forward until you feel a stretch across your chest.
