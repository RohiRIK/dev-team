# Animal Flow Basics

This document provides a basic introduction to the Animal Flow practice, which the user has experience with (**H15**) and can be used as a fun, equipment-free workout option (**S6**). The focus is on ground-based movement to improve strength, flexibility, and coordination.

---

## 1. The Philosophy of Animal Flow

Animal Flow is about reconnecting with the way our bodies are naturally meant to move. Unlike traditional, linear strength training, it's a fluid, four-dimensional practice. It's an excellent way to add a playful and skill-based component to your routine, which can help with the feeling of not enjoying home workouts anymore (**Pr5**) and supports the principle of having fun in the workout (**Fp5**).

**Benefits for you:**
- **Improved Mobility & Posture:** Directly supports **Fp1** and **Fp2**.
- **Enhanced Bodyweight Strength:** Builds functional strength without weights.
- **Better Coordination & Body Awareness:** A new skill to master, making training more engaging.

---

## 2. The ABCs: Base Positions

Every Animal Flow practice is built from three base positions. Mastering these is the first step.

1.  **Beast:** The quadrupedal position (on hands and feet). Your knees are under your hips, slightly off the ground. This is the starting point for many movements.
2.  **Crab:** The reverse of Beast. You are seated, with hands behind you and feet on the floor, and then you lift your hips slightly off the ground.
3.  **Ape:** A deep squat position, with heels on the ground and an upright torso.

---

## 3. Beginner Movements to Practice

Start by mastering these individual movements before trying to link them into a flow.

### 3.1. Wrist Mobilizations

**Why:** You will be on your hands a lot. It is critical to warm up your wrists to prevent injury.

**How:** Spend 2-3 minutes performing wrist circles, forward and backward rocks on all fours, and other gentle wrist stretches before every session.

### 3.2. Beast Crawl

**Why:** This is a fundamental crawling pattern that builds core and shoulder stability.

**How:** From the Beast position, move one hand and the opposite foot forward a small step. Repeat with the other side. Keep your hips low and your spine neutral. Aim for smooth, controlled movement.

### 3.3. Side Kick-through

**Why:** This is one of the most iconic Animal Flow movements. It builds core strength, coordination, and hip mobility.

**How:**
1.  Start in Beast.
2.  Lift one hand and the opposite foot simultaneously (e.g., right hand, left foot).
3.  Pivot on the ball of the planted foot (right foot) as you bring your lifted leg (left leg) through and across your body, extending it straight.
4.  Your torso will rotate, and you will be momentarily supported by your planted hand and foot.
5.  Return to Beast with control and repeat on the other side.

### 3.4. Crab Reach

**Why:** This is an excellent movement for opening up the hips and thoracic spine, counteracting the effects of sitting.

**How:**
1.  Start in the Crab position.
2.  Lift one hand (e.g., right hand) and drive through your feet to lift your hips high.
3.  Reach the lifted hand over and across your body, creating a full-body arch. Your head should look down at your planted hand.
4.  Return to Crab with control and repeat on the other side.
