# Research Guidelines

When building a new agent, it is important to conduct thorough research to ensure that the agent has the knowledge and tools it needs to perform its tasks. The following are some guidelines for conducting research for building an agent:

-   **Identify the agent's role and responsibilities:** The first step is to clearly define the agent's role and responsibilities. This will help to focus the research and ensure that the agent has the knowledge and tools it needs to perform its tasks.
-   **Identify the agent's target audience:** Who will be using the agent? What are their needs and expectations? This will help to tailor the agent's knowledge and tools to the target audience.
-   **Identify the agent's key tasks:** What are the key tasks that the agent will be performing? This will help to identify the knowledge and tools that the agent will need to perform its tasks.
-   **Identify existing resources:** Are there any existing resources that can be used to build the agent? This can include documentation, tutorials, and blog posts.
-   **Use a variety of sources:** Use a variety of sources to gather information, including books, articles, websites, and experts.

## Deep Research

Deep research is an iterative process of gathering, analyzing, and synthesizing information to gain a deep understanding of a topic. It is not enough to simply gather data; you must also iterate on that data to give the best data. This means that you must:

-   **Commit to at least 10 minutes of research:** To ensure that you have a good understanding of the topic, you should commit to at least 10 minutes of research before you start writing.
-   **Analyze the data:** Identify the key themes, patterns, and trends in the data.
-   **Synthesize the data:** Combine the data from different sources to create a cohesive and comprehensive picture of the topic.
-   **Iterate on the data:** Continuously refine your understanding of the topic as you gather more information.

## Output of Research

The output of your research should not just be a collection of notes. You should aim to create valuable resources that can be used by the agent and by other developers. This includes:

-   **Best Practices:** A set of guidelines and recommendations for how to perform a task.
-   **Cheatsheets:** A concise summary of the key information about a topic.
-   **Knowledge Files:** The core knowledge base for the agent.
