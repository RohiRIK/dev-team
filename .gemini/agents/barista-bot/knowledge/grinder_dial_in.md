# How to Dial In Your Varia VS3 Grinder

This document provides a step-by-step guide to dialing in your **Varia VS3 grinder (Cfs2)** for use with your **Flair 58 (Cfs1)**. Mastering this process is the most important skill for unlocking your goal of drinking good coffee (**N10**).

---

## 1. The Goal of Dialing In

The goal is to find the correct grind size that provides the right amount of resistance to the water from your Flair 58. We control this by aiming for a specific **yield** (weight of liquid espresso) in a specific **time**.

- **Starting Recipe:** 18g of dry coffee grounds (dose) -> 36g of liquid espresso (yield).
- **Target Time:** 25-35 seconds (from the moment you start pulling the lever).

Essentially, you are only changing **one variable**—the grind size on your Varia VS3—to hit the target time.

---

## 2. The Method: A Step-by-Step Process

1.  **Start with a Benchmark:** Set your Varia VS3 to a setting that is known to be in the espresso range. If you are unsure, it's better to start too **coarse** than too fine.

2.  **Dose and Prep:** Weigh exactly 18g of coffee. Grind it, and perform your puck prep as detailed in the `flair_58_guide.md`.

3.  **Pull the Shot:** Pull the shot on your Flair 58, aiming for a 36g yield. Start your timer the moment you begin to pull the lever. Stop the timer when you hit 36g of liquid in the cup.

4.  **Evaluate the Time:**
    - **Did the shot run in under 25 seconds?** Your grind is **too coarse**. The water is flowing through too easily.
    - **Did the shot run in over 35 seconds?** Your grind is **too fine**. The water is struggling to get through.
    - **Did the shot run between 25-35 seconds?** You are in the correct range! Now you can fine-tune based on taste.

5.  **Adjust and Repeat:**
    - If the shot was too fast, make a **small adjustment** on your Varia VS3 to a **finer** setting.
    - If the shot was too slow, make a **small adjustment** to a **coarser** setting.
    - **Purge the grinder** by running a couple of beans through after you adjust.
    - Repeat steps 2-4 with the new grind setting.

---

## 3. Troubleshooting by Taste

Once your shot time is in the 25-35 second range, you can make final adjustments based on taste.

- **If it tastes sour, acidic, or watery:** The shot is **under-extracted**. The water didn't have enough time to pull out the sweet flavors. **Grind slightly finer** to increase the extraction time.

- **If it tastes bitter, harsh, or burnt:** The shot is **over-extracted**. The water spent too much time with the coffee and pulled out unpleasant flavors. **Grind slightly coarser** to decrease the extraction time.

- **If it tastes sweet, balanced, and complex:** You've nailed it. Note down the grind setting on your Varia VS3 for this specific coffee bean.
