# Flair 58 Brewing Guide

This document is a comprehensive guide to brewing espresso with your Flair 58, serving as a core knowledge file for the `barista-bot` agent. It is tailored to your specific coffee stack as detailed in `TELOS.md`.

---

## 1. Your Setup

This guide assumes the use of your primary espresso equipment:
- **Espresso Machine:** Flair 58 (**Cfs1**)
- **Grinder:** Varia VS3 (**Cfs2**)
- **Puck Prep Tools:** WDT (**Cfs4**), Distributor (**Cfs18**), and Tamper (**Cfs21**)
- **Scale:** Personal Electronic Scale (**Cfs14**)

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Brewing Workflow

This workflow provides a starting point. The key to great espresso is consistency and making small, deliberate changes to one variable at a time.

1.  **Preheat:** Turn on the Flair 58 to your desired temperature setting. A good starting point is the medium setting for most beans. Lock the portafilter in place to preheat it simultaneously.

2.  **Dose & Grind:** Weigh out an 18g dose of coffee beans. Grind them with your **Varia VS3 grinder** (**Cfs2**). The initial grind setting should be fine, similar to powdered sugar, but this will be the main variable you adjust.

3.  **Puck Prep (WDT & Tamp):**
    - Place the portafilter on your **scale** (**Cfs14**) and use your **dosing funnel** (**Cfs7**) to ensure all grounds get in.
    - Use your **WDT tool** (**Cfs4**) to break up clumps and evenly distribute the grounds. The goal is a uniform, fluffy bed of coffee.
    - Gently tap the portafilter on the counter to settle the grounds.
    - Use your **distributor** (**Cfs18**) if desired, but do not press down hard. It should only level the very top layer.
    - Tamp firmly and evenly with your **IKAPE Tamper** (**Cfs21**). The pressure should be consistent every time. Place the puck screen on top.

4.  **Brewing:**
    - Lock the portafilter into the preheated brew head.
    - Place your **measuring cup** (**Cfs16**) on the scale and tare it to zero.
    - Add boiling water to the brew chamber.
    - **Pre-infusion:** Gently lower the lever to apply 1-2 bars of pressure. Hold for 5-10 seconds. You should see the first few drops of espresso appear.
    - **Ramp to Pressure:** Smoothly ramp up the pressure to 9 bars. 
    - **Extraction:** Maintain a steady 8-9 bars of pressure. The shot should flow like warm honey.
    - **Stop the Shot:** As you approach your target yield (e.g., 36g for a 1:2 ratio), begin to ramp down the pressure. Stop when you hit your target weight.

5.  **Taste and Adjust:** This is the most important step. Based on the taste, you will adjust your grind size for the next shot (see section 3).

---

## 3. Key Variables to Control

Your goal is to balance these variables to achieve a shot that is not too sour (under-extracted) and not too bitter (over-extracted). Total shot time should typically be between 25-40 seconds.

- **Grind Size (Your Primary Adjustment):**
  - **Shot too fast & tastes sour?** -> Grind finer.
  - **Shot too slow & tastes bitter?** -> Grind coarser.

- **Dose:** The amount of dry coffee grounds. 18-20g is a standard starting point. Keep this consistent while you are dialing in the grind size.

- **Yield:** The weight of the liquid espresso. A 1:2 ratio (e.g., 18g dose -> 36g yield) is a standard starting point. You can play with this later to alter the strength and flavor profile.

- **Pressure Profile:** The Flair 58 gives you full control. You can experiment with longer pre-infusions or different pressure ramps once you have a baseline recipe you are happy with.
