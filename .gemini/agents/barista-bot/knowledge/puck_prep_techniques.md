# Espresso Puck Prep Techniques

This document outlines the best practices for preparing your puck of coffee before extraction. Proper puck prep is essential for preventing channeling (uneven extraction) and getting the most flavor out of your coffee. This guide is tailored to your specific equipment.

---

## The Goal: A Uniform Puck

The entire goal of puck prep is to create a coffee bed of uniform density. This encourages water from the Flair 58 to flow through the entire puck evenly, extracting flavor consistently. Clumps, gaps, or an un-level surface will lead to channeling, where water finds an easy path, resulting in a sour and bitter (under- and over-extracted) shot.

---

## 1. WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique)

This is the most important step for creating a uniform puck.

**Tool:** Your **MUVNA Adjustable Even Coffee Stirring Distribution tool (Cfs4)**.

**Why:** Grinders, even great ones, produce clumps of coffee grounds. The WDT process breaks up these clumps and ensures every particle is separated and fluffy.

**How:**
1.  After grinding into your portafilter (using a dosing funnel, **Cfs7**, is recommended to avoid mess), insert your WDT tool.
2.  Move the needles through the entire depth of the coffee bed. Start from the bottom and work your way up.
3.  Use a combination of circular motions and simple raking motions to ensure you cover the entire area.
4.  The result should be a completely uniform, fluffy, and clump-free bed of coffee.

---

## 2. Distribution & Leveling

**Tool:** Your **Cyclone Coffee Distributor (Cfs9)** or simply your hands.

**Why:** After WDT, the surface of the coffee might be uneven. This step creates a flat, level surface for the tamper to press on.

**How:**
- **Method A (Distributor Tool):** Place your distributor tool on top of the grounds. Give it a gentle spin. **Crucially, do not press down hard.** The goal is only to level the surface, not to compress the coffee. 
- **Method B (Tapping):** A simpler method is to give the portafilter a few firm, vertical taps on the counter. This will settle the grounds into a flat bed.

Many enthusiasts find that a simple tap is just as effective, if not more so, than a distributor tool. Experiment to see what works for you.

---

## 3. Tamping

**Tool:** Your **IKAPE Espresso Tamper V5 (Cfs21)**.

**Why:** Tamping compresses the loose grounds into a dense, solid puck that can provide the necessary resistance against the 9 bars of pressure from the Flair 58.

**How:**
1.  **Level is Everything:** The most important part of tamping is to ensure the tamp is perfectly level. An un-level tamp will cause one side of the puck to be more compressed than the other, leading to severe channeling.
2.  **Consistent Pressure:** The exact amount of pressure is less important than using the *same* pressure every single time. Aim for a firm press until the coffee bed feels solid and won't compress any further.
3.  **No Twisting:** Avoid twisting the tamper at the end. This can fracture the surface of the puck and is an outdated technique.

By following these three steps, you ensure that the water has no choice but to pass through the coffee puck evenly, leading to a delicious and consistent shot of espresso every time.
